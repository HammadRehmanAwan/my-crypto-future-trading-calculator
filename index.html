Education & Risk Management

Master the fundamentals before you risk capital. Futures trading is high-risk — these principles help you survive long enough to succeed.

Risk Management Essentials

  • Risk 1–2% per trade. Never put more than a small slice of your capital on a single position — survive losing streaks.
  • Always set a stop-loss. Decide your exit before you enter. No stop = unlimited emotional risk.
  • Target 1:2 reward-to-risk or better. Win less than half your trades and still come out ahead.
  • Size down on counter-trend trades. Fighting the dominant trend needs smaller positions.
  • Never add to a losing position hoping it recovers — that's how accounts blow up.

Leverage & Liquidation

  • Leverage multiplies both ways. 10× turns a 1% move into 10% of your margin — profit and loss.
  • Liquidation is forced closure when losses exhaust your margin. At 100× a ~1% adverse move wipes you out.
  • Liquidation distance ≈ 1 / leverage. 5× ≈ 20% buffer; 25× ≈ 4%; 100× ≈ 1%.
  • Lower leverage = more survivable. Most disciplined traders stay under 10–20×.
  • Liquidation clusters act as magnets — price often hunts dense zones (see the Liquidation Heatmap).

Indicator Glossary

RSI (14)
Momentum 0–100. <30 oversold (bounce potential), >70 overbought (pullback risk).
MACD
Trend momentum. Line above signal = bullish; below = bearish.
Bollinger Bands
Volatility envelope. A squeeze often precedes a big move.
EMA 20/50/200
Trend structure. Price above all three = strong uptrend.
ATR
Average volatility — used to size stops (1.5–2× ATR).
Funding Rate
Perp payment between longs/shorts. Extreme positive often precedes corrections.
Open Interest
Total open contracts. Rising OI + rising price = conviction.
Long/Short Ratio
Crowd positioning — extremes set up squeezes.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Over-leveraging. The #1 account killer. High leverage feels powerful until one wick liquidates you.
  • Revenge trading. Chasing losses with bigger size compounds the damage.
  • No stop-loss / moving it. Widening a stop to "give it room" turns a small loss into a big one.
  • FOMO entries. Buying after a vertical pump usually means buying the top.
  • Ignoring funding & sentiment. Crowded trades reverse violently.
